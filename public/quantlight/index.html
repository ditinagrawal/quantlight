ADVANCED LIGHT RESEARCH

QuantLight & High Harmonics

Pioneering research focuses on the implications and advancement of classical and quantum structured light, uncovering how such light fields are involved in both scientific and industrial applications. It also encompasses creating ultrashort laser pulses at shorter wavelengths via high-harmonic generation or nonlinear light-matter interactions, opening possibilities and applications including EUV imaging, EUV lithography, EUV metrology, nanofabrication, coherent diffractive imaging, and shaping attosecond pulses

Our Capabilities

Cutting-Edge Studies in Structured Light & High Harmonics

Our exceptional experimental and technological capabilities allow us to innovate new frontiers in optics and photonics. Some of our expertise is listed below as follows:




Generating structured laser sources at Infrared, visible, UV, and deep UV wavelengths

Developing advanced laser systems capable of producing precisely controlled structured beams across multiple wavelengths.

High-quality diffractive optics for high-power and lossless beam shaping

Designing precision diffractive optics that enable efficient and distortion-free shaping of high-intensity laser beams.

Surface processing and structuring with vortex and vector light beams

Utilizing vortex and vector beams for micro- and nano-scale surface modification with high precision and control.

Laser drilling, cutting, and scribing with non-diffracting laser beams

Applying non-diffracting laser technology for precise and high-speed drilling, cutting, and scribing applications.

High-resolution imaging: problems and solutions

Exploring innovative optical designs and computational methods to overcome imaging limitations.

XUV metrology and XUV imaging using structured light beams

Advancing precise XUV characterization and imaging through tailored structured light sources.

Lower-order and Higher-order harmonic generation driven by classical and quantum light

Investigating harmonic generation mechanisms to extend photon energy control using quantum light sources.

Research & Citations

Exploring the Frontiers of Optical and Laser Sciences

Our research at Quantlight & High Harmonics Lab focuses on advancing the field of photonics, laser dynamics, and optical imaging. Through a blend of theory and experiment, we strive to push the boundaries of light–matter interaction, structured beams, and high-resolution optical analysis.

Recent Citations

2024 – Beam Shaping for Nonlinear Optics

A comprehensive study on adaptive beam shaping for controlling nonlinear light propagation and enhancing phase stability in high-intensity laser environments.

2023 – Structured Light in Optical Imaging

Development of vortex and spiral light fields for improving depth perception and information transfer in quantum optical systems.

2022 – Spiral Point Spread Functions

Demonstrating 3D incoherent imaging using spiral rotating point spread functions, enabling sharper image reconstruction and efficient computational optics.

2021 – Self-Healing Laser Beams

Investigation of autofocusing and self-healing properties of aberrated laser beams in turbulent media, with implications for atmospheric optical communication.
